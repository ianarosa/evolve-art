🧬 What am I watching?

Each white dot is a guess at the best location on the colored map. Brighter (yellow) areas are better; dark purple areas are worse.

Every generation, the best guesses "reproduce" with small random changes, and the worst guesses die off. Over time the whole swarm evolves toward the brightest area.

That's a genetic algorithm — evolution used as a search. Hit Play and watch the dots climb. The line at the top tells you, in plain words, what's happening.

How it works

Each dot is an individual whose genome is a point (x, y). The colored map is a fitness landscape — brighter is better. Every generation the algorithm: evaluates fitness, selects fitter parents, crosses over and mutates them into a new population.

Try the Rastrigin landscape with a low mutation rate and watch the swarm get stuck on a local peak — the diversity meter collapsing is premature convergence. Raise mutation strength to escape.
